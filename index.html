Mentoring vs. Tutoring

When students work with Dr. Hurley on a weekly basis, they receive more than just tutoring—they are mentored.

  • Mentoring is an ongoing, weekly partnership designed to provide consistent guidance, premium support, and long-term success.
  • Tutoring, on the other hand, is ideal for short-term, as-needed assistance.

For the first week, all students—regardless of their tutoring plan—gain access to the full suite of mentorship benefits (outlined below). This allows students to experience the added value of ongoing support, daily engagement, and a community of like-minded learners. After the trial period, students who commit to weekly mentoring will retain these premium benefits, while others can continue with flexible tutoring sessions tailored to their needs.

Benefits of Mentoring

Access to a Private Learning Community

Students will be invited to join an exclusive online learning group where they can collaborate with other AP Computer Science A students, share ideas, and ask questions in a supportive environment. Parents are welcome to oversee the group, and students have the option to participate anonymously if they prefer. Of course, joining the group is entirely optional.

AP-Style Question of the Day

Every day, an AP-style question will be posted in the private learning community, specifically designed to keep students engaged and actively practicing key concepts between sessions. These questions closely mirror the format and difficulty of the actual AP exam, helping students build confidence and familiarity with exam-style problems. Solutions are posted the following day for self-assessment, reinforcing learning and exam readiness.

Weekly Office Hours

Students will have access to a dedicated weekly office hour for extra support, clarification, or advice. Office hours will rotate on a weekly basis to accommodate students with varying schedules.

Guaranteed Question Support

When students get stuck on a question or concept, they can post it in the private learning community and receive a guaranteed response from Dr. Hurley within 48 hours (usually much sooner). Whether it's debugging code or understanding a tricky concept, students will never feel stuck for too long.

Peer Observation Access

Students have the option to join the Peer Observation Access program. By opting in, students agree to allow other students to observe their sessions and, in return, gain the opportunity to observe other students' sessions. Observers remain muted and do not interact during the entire session, ensuring all sessions remain focused and productive.
