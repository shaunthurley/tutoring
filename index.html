About Dr. Hurley

Dr. Shaun Hurley holds degrees in physics, civil engineering, and curriculum & instruction with a specialization in educational technology. He’s an Apple Distinguished Educator and professionally certified in computer science, math, and physics. With over 15 years of classroom teaching experience, he has taught in three public and three independent schools, primarily at the high school level but also ranging from middle school to higher education.

Current Work

He currently works as a learning experience designer for Pathstream, developing online courses primarily in the field of data analytics. His most recent project involved partnering with Coursera and Tableau to build the Business Intelligence Analyst certificate. This course has seen over 20,000 student enrollments, increasing by roughly 500 students per week.

Additional Roles

During his time as a classroom teacher, Dr. Hurley held many additional roles, including Director of Educational Technology, Math Department Chair, and Computer Science Department Chair. He has traveled the country offering week-long technology camps hosted at distinguished schools, including Stanford University, Harvard University, and George Washington University.

Dr. Hurley's experience extends beyond secondary education into higher education, where he has worked as a contractor for several universities:

  • University of Florida: He was part of a small team that developed the Computer Science Education program, designed to help teachers in Florida earn their computer science professional teaching certificate. After the certificate was developed, Dr. Hurley taught the first course in the certificate: K-12 Computer Programming Foundations.

  • University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill: He worked directly with professors to transform their face-to-face courses into hybrid and fully online formats. His work involved three of the university's largest courses, including Software Architecture, Calculus of Functions of One Variable, and Intro to Organic Chemistry I.

  • Skidmore College: He was consulted by professors to help plan the transformation of their face-to-face classes to hybrid/online environments.

Speaking Experience

Outside of his regular work, Dr. Hurley has presented at several notable conferences, including the International Society for Technology in Education (ISTE), Future of Education Technology Conference (FETC), and Computer Science Teachers Association (CSTA). He also served as the commencement speaker at St. Edward's School in 2016.
